PRIVACY POLICY

Last Updated: 20/01/2026

Barefoot Nomads ("Barefoot Nomads", "we", "us", "our") is a sole proprietorship operating under the trade name Barefoot Nomads values the trust you place in us when you choose our travel services. This Privacy Policy ("Policy") explains how we collect, use, store, disclose, transfer, and protect your personal data when you interact with our website, booking platforms, communication channels, or participate in tours and travel experiences organised or facilitated by Barefoot Nomads.

By accessing our website, submitting enquiries, making bookings, joining our trips, or otherwise engaging with our services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agreed to the practices described in this Policy.

1. Applicability

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • All customers and travellers, whether located in India or overseas, including foreign nationals, who book or participate in any tour, trek, event, or service organised or facilitated by Barefoot Nomads
  • Visitors and users who access or interact with our website, booking forms, WhatsApp communications, emails, social media platforms, or offline booking channels
  • Prospective customers or leads who provide personal data during enquiries, promotional campaigns, or marketing interactions, irrespective of whether a booking is completed
  • Parents or guardians providing information for minors and individuals listed as emergency contacts or nominees

2. Definitions

For the purpose of this Policy:

  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including name, contact details, identification documents, or online identifiers
  • "Sensitive Personal Data" includes passport details, government-issued identification, financial information, medical or health-related data, and information relating to minors
  • "Processing" means any operation performed on personal data including collection, storage, use, disclosure, transfer, or deletion
  • "Minor" means any individual below the age of 18 years

3. Collection of Personal Data

We collect personal data that you voluntarily provide while enquiring about or booking our services. This may include:

  • Full name, email address, phone number, postal address
  • Date of birth, nationality, gender
  • Passport details, visa information, and government-issued ID proofs
  • Emergency contact details
  • Travel preferences, itinerary selections, accommodation details, dietary preferences, and co-traveller information
  • Health or medical disclosures made voluntarily for travel suitability or safety, especially for adventure or high-altitude trips
  • Payment-related information (processed securely via third-party gateways)

Personal data may be collected through multiple channels including our website forms, Google Forms, WhatsApp chats, emails, phone calls, social media platforms, offline booking forms, or authorised sales representatives. For group bookings, personal data of co-travellers may be provided by the primary booking individual.

We do not knowingly purchase personal data from third-party brokers.

4. Automatically Collected Technical Data

When you interact with our digital platforms, we automatically collect certain technical and usage data including:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device identifiers and operating system
  • Approximate geolocation
  • Session duration, clickstream data, referral URLs
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

We use tools such as Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager, Meta (Facebook) Pixel, and similar technologies to analyse usage patterns, improve platform performance, measure marketing effectiveness, and enhance security.

5. Use of Cookies

Our website uses cookies, pixels, tags, and similar technologies ("Cookies") to:

  • Enable essential website functionality
  • Improve performance and user experience
  • Analyse traffic and usage behaviour
  • Deliver relevant marketing communications

You may control or disable Cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling certain Cookies may limit website functionality. Continued use of our website constitutes consent to our use of Cookies in accordance with this Policy.

6. Categories of Personal Data Collected

Barefoot Nomads may collect and process the following categories of data:

  • Contact Information: name, email address, phone number, emergency contacts
  • Identity Information: passport details, ID proofs, nationality, date of birth
  • Financial Information: limited payment references and billing details (processed via secure gateways only)
  • Travel & Booking Information: destinations, itineraries, accommodation preferences, booking history
  • Health & Medical Information: voluntarily disclosed medical conditions, allergies, fitness declarations
  • Children's Information: limited data relating to minors, collected strictly under parental/guardian consent
  • Technical & Usage Information: IP address, cookies, device and browser information

7. Purpose and Lawful Basis for Processing

We process personal data for the following purposes:

  • Facilitating and managing travel bookings and related services
  • Communicating trip details, updates, and emergency information
  • Customer support, dispute resolution, and grievance handling
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory obligations (including taxation, foreign remittance, and travel regulations)
  • Fraud prevention, security monitoring, and internal analytics
  • Marketing communications (only with consent)

The lawful bases for processing include:

  • Your consent
  • Performance of a contract with you
  • Compliance with legal obligations
  • Legitimate business interests such as security, analytics, and service improvement

You may opt out of marketing communications at any time.

8. Sharing and Disclosure of Personal Data

To deliver travel services, your personal data may be shared with third-party service providers such as airlines, hotels, transport operators, insurance providers, visa consultants, local guides, and destination management partners. These third parties may be located in India or overseas depending on the destination.

We require such third parties to process data only for service delivery and subject to confidentiality obligations. Barefoot Nomads does not sell or rent personal data.

We may disclose personal data where required by law, legal process, or regulatory authorities.

9. Cross-Border Data Transfers

For international travel services, personal data may be transferred outside India. Barefoot Nomads ensures appropriate safeguards through contractual obligations and internationally accepted data protection standards. Where applicable, principles consistent with GDPR are applied.

10. Storage, Retention, and Security

Personal data is stored on secure cloud-based servers and CRM systems with restricted access granted only to authorised personnel. We implement technical and organisational measures including encryption, access controls, monitoring systems, and secure APIs.

Data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected or as required by applicable laws including tax, accounting, or regulatory requirements.

In the event of a data breach, Barefoot Nomads shall promptly investigate, mitigate risks, and notify affected individuals and authorities as required under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023.

11. Rights of Users

You have the right to:

  • Access, review, and correct your personal data
  • Request deletion of personal data, subject to legal obligations
  • Withdraw consent for marketing communications

Where applicable, you may also request restriction of processing or data portability. Requests may be submitted via our official contact channels and will be processed after reasonable identity verification.

12. Children and Minors

Barefoot Nomads does not knowingly collect personal data of minors without verified parental or guardian consent. Data relating to minors is collected strictly for booking purposes and subject to enhanced safeguards.

13. Use of Images, Videos, and Testimonials

During tours and events, photographs or videos may be captured by Barefoot Nomads or authorised representatives for operational or promotional purposes. By participating in our trips, you consent to the reasonable use of such media on our website, social media platforms, and marketing materials.

Clients who do not wish to have their identifiable images used must notify us in writing prior to the commencement of travel.

14. Compliance with Law

Barefoot Nomads complies with applicable Indian laws including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and the Information Technology Act, 2000, along with related rules. For overseas users, equivalent international data protection principles are applied where required.

15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time to reflect changes in law, technology, or business practices. Updates will be published on our website with the revised "Last Updated" date.
