Unveiling the Heart of Barefoot Nomads

Imagine a dreamer who turned her wanderlust into a journey of laughter and adventure. That’s me, Bhumi, the mind and soul behind Barefoot Nomads. For years, I’ve dreamt of crafting a travel experience that goes beyond the ordinary—a journey where each step is as enriching as the destination itself.

With over 100 trips under my belt, I’ve seen laughter become the universal language of every adventure. I’ve spent 6+ years exploring this world I adore, soaking in every culture and landscape. For me, it’s not just about the places; it’s the people who make each journey unforgettable.

They say it’s not the destination but the company that makes the travel special. And at Barefoot Nomads, we embody that spirit. We’ve trekked through 10+ destinations, weaving together both rugged trails and leisurely escapes. I’m not just a guide; I’m the heart of a team that handles everything—from leadership and sales to execution and social media—with an unquenchable thirst for learning.

How I started BarefootNomads??

My journey into the world of adventure began during my undergraduate years studying mass communication. Early on, I discovered a deep-seated passion for exploration and traveling. This realization led me to work with several travel agencies, where I honed my skills and learned the intricacies of crafting memorable travel experiences. As time went on, I felt a growing desire to carve my own path in the industry. With a heart full of wanderlust and a mind brimming with ideas, I decided to start my own thing: Barefoot Nomads.

At Barefoot Nomads, my goal was clear—to offer both ready-made and customized adventure packages that would cater to the unique desires and dreams of each adventurer. From bustling cities to remote paradises, I aimed to create journeys that left a lasting impact. Soon, adventurers began to trust us to deliver unforgettable experiences since then It's been a journey of passion, learning, and fulfillment, proving that when you follow your dreams, incredible things can happen.
